## Executive Summary

Farrier (formerly OpStack) is a services-led technology business targeting the 2.53 million UK SMEs that recognise the potential of digital/AI transformation but lack the knowledge, technical capability, and confidence to adopt it effectively.

**Core positioning:** We are the trusted technical partner that builds operational systems for businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets but can't justify enterprise software.

---

## Market Opportunity

### The Gap (DSIT Research, Feb 2026)

| Statistic | Insight |
|-----------|---------|
| 80% of UK businesses | Neither use nor plan to use AI |
| 51% | Don't see AI as relevant to them |
| 16% | Currently using AI (mostly just ChatGPT) |
| #1 barrier | "Lack of identified need" (not cost, not skills) |
| What they want | "Tried-and-tested use cases to demonstrate value" |

**Key insight:** The market opportunity isn't "AI adoption" - it's helping businesses see that operational systems are relevant to *their* situation.

### Target Market Definition

**2.53 million UK businesses that:**
- Can accurately recognise the potential of digital/AI
- Lack the knowledge, technical capability, and confidence to adopt it
- Have outgrown spreadsheets and fragmented SaaS tools
- Cannot justify the cost, complexity, or risk of enterprise software

**They are NOT looking for:**
- "Digital transformation"
- "AI solutions"
- Another SaaS tool to manage

**They ARE looking for:**
- Someone to sort out their operational mess
- Systems that work without them becoming IT experts
- Control and oversight without complexity

## Positioning Analysis

### What We've Learned (April Dunford Review)

#### Don't Lead with AI
The DSIT research shows 51% of businesses don't see AI as relevant. Leading with "AI Workflow Delivery" or "AI needs a middleman" triggers the "not for me" response.

**Better:** Lead with operational outcomes. Mention technology as enabler, not headline.

#### The "Lack of Identified Need" Problem
This is the #1 barrier - not cost, not skills, not fear. Businesses don't connect digital systems to their specific problems.

**Solution:** Case studies, before/after examples, specific outcomes. "Reduced admin from 12 hours/week to 3."

#### Trust and Control
84% of businesses using AI want significant human oversight. Fear of "loss of personal service" is real.

**Our message must include:** "You stay in control." "Your clients chose you for a reason."

### OpStack vs Farrier Positioning

| Aspect | OpStack (Current) | Farrier (Proposed) | Recommendation |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Lead message | Operational clarity | AI adoption | Keep OpStack approach |
| Tagline | "Your business, digitised properly" | "Structured support, improved traction, peak performance" | Test both |
| Business model | Custom services | SaaS factory + services | Hybrid - see below |
| Entry point | Discovery call | AI Workflow Delivery | Need lower-commitment entry |
| Revenue | Project-based | Recurring subscriptions | Add recurring element |

## The Farrier Model (Refined)

### Three-Tier Approach

#### Tier 1: Operational Audit (Entry Level)
*Formerly "AI Workflow Delivery" - reframed away from AI*

- Fixed-price operational assessment
- Map current processes, identify 3 highest-impact improvements
- Deliverable: Roadmap document + recommendation
- **Price point:** £500-1,000
- **Purpose:** Low-risk entry, qualifies for Tier 2/3

#### Tier 2: Foundation Build (Core Offering)
*Custom operational system build*

- 4-8 week engagement
- Core operational system addressing primary pain point
- Training and handover included
- **Price point:** £5,000-15,000
- **Optional:** Ongoing support retainer (£500-1,500/month)

#### Tier 3: Full Digital Operating Layer (Strategic)
*Complete operational transformation*

- 3-6 month engagement
- Multiple integrated systems
- Ongoing partnership model
- **Price point:** £20,000-50,000+
- **Revenue share or licensing options available**

### Off-the-Shelf Products (Future Consideration)

The Farrier plan proposes building "industry-specific software products built once and deployed at scale."

**Pros:**
- Recurring revenue
- Scalable
- Lower marginal cost per customer

**Cons:**
- Requires upfront R&D investment
- Different skillset than custom delivery
- Product-market fit risk per vertical

**Decision needed:** Do we pursue this? If so, which vertical first?
